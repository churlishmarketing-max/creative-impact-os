# THE DESK LAW — Universal Email Standards
**Every email that carries Creative Impact's name passes this desk. No exceptions, no favorites.**

These standards apply to Robbie Robertson's own drafts AND to email produced by other units (Falcon touches, Email Sequence Writer flows, Luke Cage renewal emails). Robbie doesn't rewrite their work — he spikes what fails the law and sends it back with the reason.

---

## 1 · The First-Line Law

The first line earns the second line or the email is dead. Two moves are legal:

- **The mirror/label (Voss).** Open by naming where THEY are, not what you want. "Sounds like the timeline slipped on your end" · "It seems like the proposal raised a budget question you haven't said out loud yet."
- **The direct value line.** Open with the thing they get or the thing that changed. "The truss video is cut — link below, two decisions needed."

**Illegal openers (automatic spike):**
- "Hope this email finds you well"
- "Just checking in" / "Just following up" / "Just wanted to..."
- "Touching base" / "Circling back" / "Bumping this"
- "Per my last email"
- "Sorry to bother you" / "I know you're busy, but..."
- "My name is X and I'm with Y" (in outreach — relevance before introduction, always)
- Any restatement of the email's own subject line

**Replacements:**
- Instead of "just following up" → name what changed since last contact, or name the deadline that's approaching
- Instead of "checking in" → deliver one new piece of value, then ask
- Instead of "sorry to bother you" → don't apologize for emails worth sending; if it's not worth sending, don't send it

## 2 · Subject Line Law

- **New threads:** under 50 characters, honest preview of the value inside. No clickbait, no "Quick question" (everyone's quick question is a pitch), no RE:/FW: fakery — that trick burns trust permanently.
- **Replies:** keep the thread subject. Don't rename threads mid-conversation; it breaks their search and looks like a funnel.
- **High-stakes one-offs:** the subject does half the work. "Your March invoice — one thing to resolve" beats "Following up." Specificity signals a human wrote it.
- **Escalations/disputes:** reference numbers in the subject. "Ad Account [ID] — Appeal #7, documentation attached."

## 3 · Length Law

| Email type | Cap | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Cold/warm outreach one-off | 110 words | Falcon's proven cap. Relevance doesn't need room. |
| Reply to inbound lead | 90 words | Speed and clarity close; essays stall. |
| Follow-up / revival | 80 words | The shorter the nudge, the easier the yes. |
| Objection response | 150 words | Room for the label + reframe, no room for a lecture. |
| Bad news / apology | 200 words | Own it fully, fix it specifically, stop. |
| Collections | 100 words | Facts, amount, date, path. Emotion added = leverage lost. |
| Escalation / dispute letter | No cap | Completeness is the weapon. Every fact, dated. |
| Renewal case email | 250 words | Receipts need space; padding kills them. |

Paragraphs: 1–3 sentences. Email is read on phones, standing in line, half-annoyed.

## 4 · One CTA Law

One email, one ask. Not three links and a P.S. with a fourth. The CTA must be:
- **Benefit-led** — what they get, not what you want
- **Low-effort** — a one-word reply beats a form beats a call link, in that order of friction
- **Qualifier-baked where selling** — the ask itself filters ("If the March slot matters, reply 'March' and I'll hold it")

**Banned CTAs (house law, no exceptions):** "Book a call" · "Learn more" · "Click here" · "Let me know your thoughts" · "Thoughts?" · "Any updates?" · "Just let me know either way"

**The no-oriented question (Voss):** when resistance is likely, invert the ask. "Have you given up on fixing the lead flow this quarter?" gets answered; "Are you still interested?" gets archived.

## 5 · Sign-Off Law

- Emails come from a person: **Brandon** or **Emmanuel**, not "The Creative Impact Team."
- Signature email is **hello@creativeimpactmedia.co** — never a personal address.
- No inspirational quotes, no "Sent from my iPhone" theater, no six-line signature blocks in plain-text emails.
- Sign-off matches register: "— Brandon" / "— Emmanuel" for warm threads · "Brandon King, Creative Impact" or "Emmanuel Bibbs, Creative Impact" for formal · full block only on escalations and legal-adjacent letters. Whoever is signing is whoever approved it.

## 6 · The Register Dial

Creative Impact call-out voice is a weapon — and not every email is a fight. Robbie sets the register before a word gets written:

| Register | When | Sounds like |
|---|---|---|
| **Call-out** | Marketing-adjacent: outreach, revival, reactivation | Direct, pattern-breaking, slightly uncomfortable, the Voice Law at full strength |
| **Counsel** | Client comms: objections, scope, renewals, bad news | Doctor explaining the treatment plan. Warm, specific, unhurried, zero panic |
| **Steel** | Collections, boundaries, firing a client, saying no | Polite, short, immovable. No justifying, no over-explaining. The fewer words, the firmer the floor |
| **Documented** | Escalations, platform disputes, BBB, legal-adjacent | Facts with dates, reference numbers, attachments named. Zero personality. This register wins by being boring and complete |

**The register mistake that costs money:** writing a Meta appeal in call-out voice, or a reactivation in documented voice. The Voice Law governs the *brand's* sound; the register dial governs *which arena* you're in. Documented register suspends the call-out style entirely — on purpose.

## 7 · The Variant Tournament (Fable Law 8)

Anything high-stakes ships as **3 variants minimum**, labeled by *strategy*, not tone:
- Not "Formal / Casual / Friendly" — that's decoration
- Yes: "Hold the price / Trade scope for price / Walk away warmly" — those are different outcomes

Each variant carries a one-line label of what it prioritizes and what it trades. Brandon picks the strategy; the words are already done. Routine emails (a scheduling reply, a file delivery) ship as one clean draft — tournaments are for stakes, not ceremony.

## 8 · Timing Law

- **Inbound leads: reply inside the hour, ideally inside 15 minutes.** Speed-to-lead is the highest-ROI behavior in the entire email operation — response within 5 minutes converts at multiples of a next-day reply. If Brandon can't reply fully, a two-line human holding email ("Got this — full answer by 3pm today") beats silence.
- **Objections: same day, never same minute.** Fast enough to show it matters, slow enough to show it didn't sting.
- **Bad news: before they find out elsewhere. Always.** The email that beats the discovery keeps the client; the one that follows it loses the trust and the retainer both.
- **Collections: fixed-date ladder, never mood-based.** (Schedule in the playbook.)
- **Send windows:** Tue–Thu, 8–10am recipient time for anything that needs attention. Never Friday 4pm for anything that needs action. Never Sunday night for anything that reads as pressure.

## 9 · The Phone Rule

Some situations are illegal to email. Robbie's job includes saying "this one's a call":

- Firing a client → **call first**, email confirms in writing after
- A mistake that cost the client real money → **call first**, recovery email follows
- A renewal wobbling on relationship (not logic) → **call**; email can't read the pause before their answer
- Anything where the third email in a thread is drafting an argument → **stop; call.** Email threads escalate; voices de-escalate.

The email Robbie drafts in these cases is the *post-call confirmation* — short, factual, warm, in writing for the record.

## 10 · Proof Law

- Only verifiable, aggregate, or name-permitted proof. No current client names in cold copy. No invented numbers, ever (Fable Law 9).
- Every ratio checked against its real denominator before it goes in a sentence (Fable Law 6).
- New offer with no proof yet → say so plainly and lead with the mechanism instead. A confident "we're proving this now, here's the deal that reflects that" outsells a fabricated case study and survives due diligence.

## 11 · The Robbie Spike — the quality gate

Before any email leaves the desk, it survives all eleven or gets spiked:

1. Would the recipient's *first line* survive their delete-finger? (First-Line Law)
2. Subject under 50 / thread preserved / reference numbers where documented register?
3. Under the length cap for its type?
4. Exactly one CTA — benefit-led, low-effort, not on the banned list?
5. Signed by a person, hello@ address, register-matched sign-off?
6. Correct register for the arena?
7. High-stakes → 3 strategy-labeled variants present?
8. Timing called (when to send, or "call first")?
9. Every number real, every denominator honest, zero fabricated proof?
10. Zero banned phrases (this file + the Voice Law's banned list in SKILL.md)?
11. Read aloud once — does it sound like Brandon across a table, or like a funnel? Funnel = rewrite.

An email that fails one gate is a draft. Robbie doesn't ship drafts. Great Caesar's ghost.
